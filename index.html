Termite Baiting Systems

In certain situations, or as a supplementary measure, we deploy highly effective termite baiting systems. Signs of Termite Infestation:

Mud Tubes

Subterranean termites build distinctive mud tubes on walls, foundations, or wooden beams. These pencil-sized tunnelsare constructed from soil, wood, and saliva and serve as protected pathways for termites to travel between theirunderground nests and your home’s wooden structures. They protect termites from predators and dry environments. Oftenfound in hidden areas like basements, crawl spaces, or behind siding, these tubes are a definitive sign of activetermite presence and indicate a need for immediate inspection for home pest control measures. Finding these mudtunnels suggests that a significant colony is already established and actively foraging within your property, andthis means you need help right away! Discarded Wings

After a termite swarm (when reproductive termites, called alates, leave the colony to establish new ones), youmight find small, translucent wings resembling fish scales near windowsills, doors, or light sources. Termites shedtheir wings soon after swarming. The presence of these wings, even in small numbers, indicates a nearby, maturetermite colony actively seeking to expand, making immediate termite control crucial. These shed wings area tell-tale sign that these wood destroying pests have been active and are trying to infest your structure, or have alreadyinfested your structure. Don’t let these termites take over your home, if you see signs of theseflying ants, contact our pest solutions team for pest services and general pest control to protect your home withpest management solutions and excellent customer service. Our pest services are designed to be reliable, effective, and efficient.

Damaged Wood

Termites consume cellulose, leaving behind hollowed-out wood. This damage often goes unnoticed until it’s severe,as termites eat wood from the inside out, preserving the outer surface. You might notice blistered or darkened wood,or hear a hollow sound when tapping on affected areas. This often manifests as tunnels, known as galleries, within thetimber. Advanced damage can lead to structural weakness, causing floors to sag, doors and windows to stick, or evenwall collapses. Any visible structural damage or unusual sounds within your wooden structures warrant an immediateprofessional pest inspection to assess the extent of the infestation and to remove all of these unwanted bugs. We canalso help you with other unwanted pests! Our pest control company can eliminate all pests from your home. We offerthe best pest control in Houma, LA!

Frass (Termite Droppings)

Drywood termites, unlike subterranean termites, do not build mud tubes. Instead, they kick out their fecal pellets,known as frass, from small kick-out holes in infested wood. Frass resembles tiny, sandy pellets, often found in smallpiles on floors or surfaces directly below damaged wood. The color of the frass can vary depending on the type of woodconsumed. Finding these granular droppings indicates a drywood termite infestation, necessitating specific pest control to eradicatethe colony and prevent further damage. These pellets are a clear sign of active drywood termite infestation,requiring targeted local pest control strategies from a trusted pest control company to prevent extensive damage. Thesetermites can be one of the most difficult to detect until it is too late, but our pest control solutions are designedto protect you from these wood destroying insects. Clicking Sounds in Walls

In very quiet environments, you might hear faint clicking or rustling sounds coming from within walls or woodenstructures. These sounds are produced by termites, specifically soldier termites, banging their heads against thewood or shaking their bodies to signal danger to the rest of the colony. While difficult to pinpoint, these sounds canbe an eerie indicator of a substantial hidden infestation, prompting an immediate professional pest control service.These sounds are a rare but chilling indication of a severe, active infestation by thesewood-eating insects, necessitating urgent professional intervention to mitigate further structural compromise of your home.Our pest services are designed to eliminate all pests.
