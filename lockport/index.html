Comprehensive Termite Treatment Options

Houma Termite offers a range of effective treatment options to eliminate termites and protect your property. The best approach depends on the type of termite, the extent of the infestation, and your specific needs.

Liquid Termite Barriers:

Liquid termiticides are a common and effective method for creating a protective barrier around your home's foundation. These treatments kill termites on contact and also have repellent properties, preventing termites from entering the treated area.

How it Works: A trench is typically dug around the perimeter of the structure, and the liquid termiticide is applied into the trench and around the foundation. This creates a continuous treated zone that termites cannot breach.

Benefits: Provides immediate protection, long-lasting residual effects, and is effective against subterranean termites.

Termite Baiting Systems:

Termite baiting systems are an environmentally friendly and highly effective solution for eliminating entire termite colonies. These systems involve placing bait stations around the property.

How it Works: Termites forage for food and discover the bait stations. They consume the bait, which contains a slow-acting insecticide, and carry it back to the colony, sharing it with other termites. This process eventually leads to the elimination of the entire colony, including the queen.

Benefits: Eliminates the colony, not just individual termites; minimal disruption to your property; provides ongoing monitoring for termite activity.

Direct Wood Treatments:

For localized infestations of drywood or dampwood termites, direct wood treatments may be employed. These treatments focus on treating specific areas of infested wood.

How it Works: Depending on the situation, this may involve injecting liquid termiticide directly into termite galleries within the wood or applying foam treatments to affected areas.

Benefits: Targets specific problem areas, effective for localized infestations, less disruptive than full-structure treatments.

Common Termite Problems and Signs You Need Service

Recognizing the signs of a termite infestation early is crucial for minimizing damage and the cost of repairs. Many homeowners in Lockport, LA may be unaware they have a termite problem until significant damage has occurred. Here are some common issues that indicate you might need professional termite control services:

Swarming Termites or Discarded Wings

Seeing winged termites, also known as swarmers, emerging from walls or foundations, or finding discarded wings near windowsills, doors, or light sources, is a strong indicator of an active termite colony nearby. Swarmers are reproductive termites that leave the colony to establish new ones. Their presence often signifies a mature colony is actively infesting your property or a neighboring one.

Why it Matters: A swarm is essentially an attempt by a termite colony to expand. Seeing swarmers means a colony is well-established and actively reproducing, increasing the likelihood of significant damage.

How Our Service Resolves it: We can quickly identify the source of the swarm and implement targeted treatments to eliminate the existing colony and prevent future swarms from originating from your property.

Mud Tubes on Walls or Foundations

Subterranean termites, the most common type in Louisiana, build protective tunnels called mud tubes. These tubes, typically the width of a pencil, are made of soil, wood, and saliva, and allow termites to travel safely between their underground colony and their food source (your home's wood structure). You might find these tubes on exterior walls, foundation elements, or even interior walls.

Why it Matters: Mud tubes are irrefutable evidence that subterranean termites are actively foraging on your property and have a direct pathway to your home's wood.

How Our Service Resolves it: We locate the mud tubes to find access points and apply treatments to eliminate the termites using the tubes and intercept foraging termites from the colony, often utilizing liquid barriers and/or baiting systems.

Hollow-Sounding or Damaged Wood

Termites eat wood from the inside out, leaving a thin veneer. If you tap on wooden structures like doorframes, baseboards, or walls, and they sound hollow, it could be a sign of termite damage. You might also notice blistering or darker areas on wood surfaces.

Why it Matters: Hollow-sounding wood indicates that the structural integrity of the wood has been compromised by termite feeding. This damage can weaken the structure of your home over time.

How Our Service Resolves it: Our inspections can confirm termite damage and determine the extent of the infestation. We then apply treatments to eliminate the termites responsible and advise on repairs to the damaged wood.

Frass (Termite Droppings)

Drywood termites leave behind tiny, granular pellets called frass. These droppings are often found near infested wood, such as on floors, windowsills, or furniture. Their color can vary depending on the type of wood the termites are consuming.

Why it Matters: Frass is a clear indication of a drywood termite infestation, which can cause significant damage within localized areas of your home's wooden structures, including furniture.

How Our Service Resolves it: We pinpoint the location of the drywood termite infestation based on the presence of frass and apply targeted treatments directly to the infested wood or utilize fumigation for widespread infestations.

Tight-Fitting Doors or Windows

As termites consume wood, the wood can swell or warp slightly. This can cause doors or windows to become difficult to open or close properly. While this symptom can also be caused by moisture issues, it is worth investigating for potential termite activity, especially if accompanied by other signs.

Why it Matters: Swollen or warped wood could be a sign of termite damage within wall frames or window/door frames, indicating an active infestation within those areas.

How Our Service Resolves it: Our thorough inspections can differentiate between termite damage and other issues. If termites are the cause, we treat the infested areas to eliminate the colony and prevent further damage.

Clicking Sounds in Walls

In quiet environments, you might hear faint clicking sounds emanating from within your walls. This sound can sometimes be produced by soldier termites banging their heads against the tunnel walls to signal danger or communicate with other termites.

Why it Matters: While not the most definitive sign, clicking sounds can be an indicator of an active and potentially large termite colony within your walls.

How Our Service Resolves it: If we confirm termite activity in your walls, we utilize targeted treatments to eradicate the colony and protect the surrounding wooden structures.

Termite Wings

Even if you don't see live swarmers, finding discarded termite wings, which resemble tiny, translucent wings, is a strong indicator. Termites shed their wings after swarming. These wings are often found in spiderwebs, on windowsills, or near light fixtures.

Why it Matters: Discarded wings are a clear sign that a termite swarm has occurred, meaning there is likely an active colony either on your property or very close by, posing an immediate threat.

How Our Service Resolves it: Upon finding discarded wings, we perform a comprehensive inspection to locate the source of the swarm and implement treatments to eliminate the colony that produced the swarm.

Termite Damage to Furniture

While many focus on structural damage, termites can also infest wooden furniture. If you notice small holes, galleries, or frass on your furniture, it could be a sign of a drywood termite infestation.

Why it Matters: Termite damage to furniture can be costly and sentimental. It also indicates that drywood termites are present on your property and could be infesting other wooden items.

How Our Service Resolves it: We can treat infested furniture or advise on appropriate treatment methods, such as fumigation if the infestation is widespread within multiple pieces of furniture.

Subterranean Termite Colonies

These are the most common and destructive termites in Lockport, LA. They live in colonies underground and build mud tubes to access food sources (wood) above ground.

Why it Matters: Subterranean termites can cause extensive damage hidden from view, often for years before signs become apparent.

How Our Service Resolves it: Our treatment methods, including liquid barriers and baiting systems, are specifically designed to target and eliminate subterranean termite colonies.

Drywood Termite Colonies

Unlike subterranean termites, drywood termites do not need contact with the soil. They infest dry wood and are often found in attics, eaves, and wooden furniture.

Why it Matters: Drywood termites can infest localized areas and cause significant damage to specific wooden elements within your home.

How Our Service Resolves it: We use targeted treatments, such as direct wood injections or fumigation, depending on the extent of the drywood termite infestation.

Formosan Termites

Formosan termites are an invasive and highly aggressive species found in Louisiana. They build large colonies and can cause rapid and severe damage.

Why it Matters: Formosan termites are known for their voracious appetite and ability to infest diverse materials, making them a particularly challenging pest.

How Our Service Resolves it: We have specialized knowledge and treatment methods for dealing with Formosan termites, ensuring effective control of this aggressive species.

Signs of Termites in Walls

Identifying termites within walls can be challenging as they often work unseen behind drywall. Look for signs like blistering paint, sagging drywall, small holes in drywall, or the presence of mud tubes or frass along baseboards or where walls meet the floor.

Why it Matters: Termites in walls can cause significant structural damage and compromise the integrity of your home's framework.

How Our Service Resolves it: We utilize specialized tools and techniques to detect termite activity within walls and implement targeted treatments to eliminate the infestation within these hidden areas.

Signs of Termites in Furniture

Inspecting wooden furniture for signs of termites, such as small holes, galleries, or frass, is important, especially for antique or outdoor furniture. Listen for faint chewing sounds in quiet environments.

Why it Matters: Termites can damage valuable furniture and potentially spread to other wooden items in your home.

How Our Service Resolves it: We can treat infested furniture or advise on appropriate treatment methods depending on the severity and type of termite infestation.

Signs of Termites in the Yard

Termite activity isn't limited to your home. Look for signs in your yard, such as mud tubes on fences, trees, or woodpiles. The presence of termite nests (carton nests) in trees or on the ground can also indicate a problem.

Why it Matters: Termites in your yard can eventually find their way into your home. Addressing outdoor termite activity is crucial for preventing infestations within your structure.
